About me

I am Reza Mostowfizadeh-Ghalamfarsa, serving as a Professor in the Department of Plant Protection at Shiraz University. My academic journey led me to attain the position of full professor in 2016, following the completion of my Ph.D. and MSc in Plant Pathology at Shiraz University, alongside my BSc in Plant Protection. Throughout my academic career, I have been honored with prestigious scholarships, including those from SCRI (now: The James Hutton Institute) in the UK, and the Centre for Phytophthora Science and Management at Murdoch University, Australia. With over 25 years of experience in plant pathology projects on both national and international scales, my expertise lies in molecular phylogenetics, diagnostics, and the detection of plant pathogenic oomycetes and ascomycetes. My contributions have extended to various research initiatives aimed at understanding and mitigating plant diseases. Additionally, I have had the privilege of serving as the Vice Dean for academic affairs and graduate studies from 2014 to 2018 at the School of Agriculture, Shiraz University. This role allowed me to contribute significantly to the academic development and research activities within the institution. My academic pursuits are fueled by a commitment to advancing the field of plant pathology and contributing to global agricultural sustainability through research, teaching, and collaboration.

Areas of Expertise

  • Fungal Plant Pathology

    Specialized in the study of fungal diseases affecting plants, including their diagnosis, management, and impact on agriculture.

  • Taxonomy of Plant Pathogenic Fungi

    Expertise in the classification and identification of plant pathogenic fungi, contributing to understanding their diversity and evolution.

  • Teaching Plant Pathology

    Extensive experience in teaching plant pathology courses at undergraduate and graduate levels, providing students with comprehensive knowledge and practical skills.

  • Academic Leadership

    Leadership in academic institutions, including serving as the Deputy of Education, leading research groups, and mentoring graduate students.

Teaching Courses

  • Advanced Mycology

    This course covers advanced concepts in mycology and aspects of fungal diversity and applied studies.

  • Genetics of Plant Pathogens

    This course explores the principles of genetics and their relation to plant pathogens, including plant resistance and susceptibility to diseases.

  • Current Topics in Mycology

    This course examines advanced and current topics in mycology, including molecular techniques and new research areas.

  • Principles of Plant Disease Management

    This course introduces the principles and methods of managing plant diseases, including biological and chemical disease control.
